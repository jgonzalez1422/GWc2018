Pueblo Magico, Guanajuato

This small town known as Dolores when the revolutionary leader, Miguel Hidalgo, uttered his famous cry for the independence of Mexico (el Grito de Dolores) in the early hours of September 16, 1810, in front of Nuestra Señora de los Dolores parroquia (parish church). The central square of the town, in front of that church, is a popular tourist spot. After Mexico achieved independence, the town was renamed Dolores Hidalgo in his honor. Today, Dolores Hidalgo, aka El Pueblo Magico, is known for its ceramics industry and that provides for over half the city's population. Area: 1,590 km² (613.9 sq mi) Elevation: 1,980 meters (6,480 feet) Population: 148,173
